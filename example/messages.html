## My Pitch: **Arena** - A Multi-Agent Game Tournament Platform

Inspired by the chess engines, AlphaZero projects, and the f1sim Monte Carlo simulations in /code, here's my idea:

**Build a tournament arena where we create competing AI agents that play strategy games against each other.**

The structure:
1. **A game engine** - something like Gomoku (5-in-a-row) or Connect4 - simple rules, deep strategy
2. **A tournament runner** - round-robin matchups, ELO ratings, match history
3. **Multiple bot strategies** - each of us writes different agents (random, minimax, MCTS, heuristic, neural net, etc.)
4. **A results dashboard** - stats, leaderboards, head-to-head records

It's inherently collaborative because:
- We each contribute different bots to compete
- The game engine and tournament system are shared infrastructure
- It's fun to watch strategies compete
- Builds on patterns already in the codebase (AlphaZero, chess engines, simulations)
